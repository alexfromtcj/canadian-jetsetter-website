Flying Blue Promo: Buy Miles with Up to 45% Off!

If you’ve been eyeing a Flying Blue redemption and just need a few more miles to make it happen, this one might be worth a look.

For a limited time, the Air France KLM Flying Blue program is offering up to 45% off when you purchase miles.

Read on to learn more. Or, if you're ready to purchase some miles, click the button below!

The Offer

Flying Blue is currently running a promotion that allows you to buy miles at a discount of up to 45 percent. This offer is available until March 5, 2026, giving travellers a few weeks to take advantage of the savings.

At the highest discount level, the cost comes down to approximately 2.2 cents CAD per mile, which can make topping up your account much more attractive, especially if you are just a few thousand miles short of a redemption.

It is worth noting that this promotion works on a tiered structure, meaning that larger purchases unlock the biggest discounts.

When Does This Make Sense?

Buying miles is not something I generally recommend without a clear plan. However, there are a few situations where it can absolutely make sense:

  • You are just short of a redemption and need to top up your balance
  • You have already found award availability and confirmed pricing
  • The cost of buying miles is significantly lower than paying cash for the same flight

Flying Blue can offer strong value on transatlantic routes, especially from Eastern Canada.

February Flying Blue Promo Rewards

This miles promo pairs nicely with Flying Blue’s February Promo.

For February, Flying Blue is offering 25% off Economy redemptions from Montreal and Toronto* to Europe, which brings the cost down to 18,750 Flying Blue miles one way + fees.

For travellers based in Eastern Canada, that can translate into some genuinely solid redemption opportunities, particularly during off-peak periods.

If you can combine discounted award pricing with strategically purchased miles, the math can work out very nicely.

I wrote a full blog post here diving deeper into the current Promo Rewards and how to maximize them, so be sure to check that out if you want the detailed breakdown.

*Note for Toronto: this promo is redeemable if you're a Flying Blue Extra member.

Quick Tip: Boost Your Flying Blue Balance with AMEX

By the way, if you collect AMEX Membership Rewards points in Canada, you can also transfer them to Flying Blue at a full 1:1 rate.

This is a simple way to top up your balance instead of buying miles, or to combine with a small purchase during the 45% off sale.

Some standout AMEX Credit Card options for earning points efficiently include:

  • American Express Platinum Card – great for frequent flyers and lounge access
  • American Express Cobalt Card – excellent for everyday spending like dining and groceries
  • American Express Gold Card – solid all-around option for flexible point earning

Think of it as a bonus tool in your Flying Blue toolkit.

Strong Business Class Sweet Spots

Flying Blue has always had excellent redemption opportunities. A few standout examples:

Vancouver to Taipei in Business Class on China Airlines

  • Around 61,000 Flying Blue miles one way
  • Roughly 13 hours of flying
  • Lie-flat seat, premium dining, and lounge access

Direct flights to Europe on Air France or KLM
Flying Blue shines on routes from:

  • Toronto: Through Paris or Amsterdam
  • Montreal: Through Paris or Amsterdam
  • Vancouver: Through Paris or Amsterdam
  • Ottawa: Through Paris
  • Calgary: Through Amsterdam
  • Edmonton: Through Amsterdam

Business Class awards on Air France or KLM often price around 60,000 miles one way + fees, which is highly competitive for transatlantic travel. Factor in monthly Promo Rewards, which can bring this figure down to 45,000 miles one way + fees, and Flying Blue becomes one of the most practical programs for Canadians.

A Few Things to Keep in Mind

Before purchasing any miles, always:

  • Search for award space first
  • Confirm total taxes and fees
  • Compare the redemption cost versus a paid fare
  • Factor in flexibility and cancellation rules

Buying miles speculatively without a booking in mind is rarely a good strategy. Flexibility is key, and availability can disappear quickly.

The Bottom Line

This Flying Blue promotion offering up to 45% off purchased miles can be a useful tool for the right traveller.

Pair it with AMEX point transfers if you have Membership Rewards, and you have more ways than ever to strategically top up your Flying Blue account.

If you are close to a redemption and have already confirmed availability, buying miles at around 2.2 cents CAD, or transferring points from AMEX, could unlock serious value.

For Eastern Canadian travellers in particular, this is a promotion worth considering.

Happy redeeming!
