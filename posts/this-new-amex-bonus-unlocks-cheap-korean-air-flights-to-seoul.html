This New Amex Bonus Unlocks Cheap Korean Air Flights to Seoul

Every once in a while, a points opportunity comes along that feels a little… hidden.

Not flashy. Not obvious. But if you know where to look, it unlocks something seriously valuable.

Right now, that opportunity is quietly sitting in the form of a new transfer bonus and it can take you all the way to Seoul, South Korea on Korean Air for a fraction of the usual cost.

Let’s walk through why this matters and how to actually take advantage of it.

The Transfer Bonus That Unlocks It All

American Express Canada is currently offering a 25% transfer bonus to Flying Blue, the loyalty program of Air France and KLM Royal Dutch Airlines.

On paper, this might look like a Europe-focused promotion. But here’s the key: Flying Blue partners with Korean Air. That’s where things get interesting.

Because instead of just thinking Paris or Amsterdam, you can start thinking Seoul. And suddenly, this bonus becomes a backdoor to Asia.

The Korean Air Sweet Spot

Through Flying Blue, you can redeem miles on Korean Air flights departing from Canada.

And the pricing right now is extremely compelling:

  • Vancouver to Seoul from ~31,000 miles one way + fees
  • Toronto to Seoul from ~40,000 miles one way + fees

With the 25% transfer bonus, those numbers get even better.

Here’s what that looks like in real terms:

  • A round-trip flight from Vancouver to Seoul can cost as little as 49,600 American Express points total.
  • A round-trip flight from Toronto to Seoul can cost as little as 64,000 American Express points total.

That is not a typo.

For a long-haul flight to Asia on a full-service airline like Korean Air, that’s an exceptional redemption.

Why This Is Such a Big Deal

Let’s ground this in real-world pricing.

For example, cash fares for nonstop Korean Air flights between Vancouver and Seoul often hover around $1,600 CAD or more.

So when you redeem ~49,600 points for that same flight, you’re getting around 3 cents per point in value on an economy ticket.

That’s the kind of value we usually associate with business class redemptions.

Seeing it here, on a transpacific economy flight, is what makes this so special.

Why Korean Air Makes This Even Better

Not all airline partners are created equal and this is where Korean Air really shines.

When you book with them, you’re getting:

  • A full-service long-haul experience
  • Solid seat comfort in economy
  • Reliable service and a strong reputation globally
  • Nonstop routes from key Canadian gateways like Vancouver and Toronto

So you’re not just saving points. You’re also getting a quality flight experience across the Pacific.

How Canadians Can Actually Do This

One of the best parts about this opportunity is how accessible it is.

Membership Rewards points are relatively easy to earn in Canada, especially with cards like:

  • The American Express Platinum Card, which currently offers a substantial welcome bonus of up to 100,000 Membership Reward Points that gets you quickly to a long-haul redemption

  • The American Express Gold Card, which also has an amazing welcome bonus of up to 90,000 Membership Reward Points, getting you amazing value when you transfer to Flying Blue Miles.

  • The American Express Cobalt Card, which currently has a welcome bonus of up to 15,000 Membership Reward Points. While not an outstanding bonus, this card continues to be one of the best earners in the country, particularly with its 5x points on food and dining. If you spend strategically, you'll earn a substantial amount of points in no time.

With the 25% transfer bonus in play, every point you earn stretches significantly further toward a Korean Air redemption.

A Few Smart Things to Keep in Mind

Before jumping in, there are a few important considerations:

  • Transfers must be made in increments of 1,000 points
  • Once transferred to Flying Blue, they cannot be reversed
  • You’ll need a Flying Blue account set up ahead of time
  • Award availability on Korean Air can vary, especially on popular dates

The biggest tip here: search for availability first, then transfer your points.

Final Thoughts

This is one of those “in the know” redemptions that doesn’t scream for attention, but absolutely delivers if you act on it.

A simple 25% transfer bonus has quietly unlocked:

  • Affordable long-haul travel to Asia
  • Excellent value on Korean Air flights
  • And a new way for Canadians to stretch their points further than usual

The Flying Blue Miles promotion runs until April 17, 2026, so there is a limited window to take advantage.

If South Korea has been on your list, this might just be the smartest way to get there.
