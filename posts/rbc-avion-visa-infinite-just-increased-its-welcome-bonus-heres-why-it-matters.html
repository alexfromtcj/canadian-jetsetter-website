RBC Avion Visa Infinite Just Increased Its Welcome Bonus: Here’s Why It Matters

Canadians, heads up. One of the most versatile travel credit cards in the country just increased its welcome bonus, and if you value flexible airline points, this is one worth paying attention to.

Until June 15, 2026, the RBC Avion Visa Infinite Card is currently offering up to 55,000 Avion points:

  • 35,000 points upon approval
  • An additional 20,000 points after spending $5,000 within six months

That is near the top end of what we typically see from Avion.

But the real story here is not just the size of the bonus. It is what you can do with those points.

Learn more by reading below, or head straight to the credit card page using the following link.

RBC Avion Visa Infinite Card

Why RBC Avion Points Are So Valuable

I have always been a big fan of the Avion program because of its flexibility.

Unlike fixed-value travel programs, Avion points can be transferred to airline loyalty programs, which is where the outsized value lives. Instead of redeeming points at a fixed rate through a bank portal, you can move them into frequent flyer programs and unlock sweet spots, premium cabins, and long-haul redemptions.

With RBC Avion, you can transfer to:

  • British Airways Executive Club (Avios)
  • Cathay Pacific Asia Miles
  • American Airlines AAdvantage
  • WestJet Rewards

For Canadians, that flexibility is extremely powerful. We do not get nearly as many transfer bonus opportunities as U.S. cardholders, so when we do, they matter.

The 30% British Airways Transfer Bonus Sweet Spot

One of my favourite recurring opportunities in the Avion ecosystem is the 30% transfer bonus to British Airways Avios.

When this promotion runs, every 1 Avion point becomes 1.3 Avios.

That is a meaningful boost. A 55,000-point welcome bonus could effectively become 71,500 Avios during a 30% transfer bonus window.

These bonuses appeared this past spring and fall, with the most recent one running from November to December 2025. While bonuses are never guaranteed, the consistency makes this program especially attractive for strategic planners.

Avios can be incredibly useful for:

  • Short‑haul flights using the distance‑based chart
  • Award redemptions with Oneworld partners
  • Strategic transatlantic or long‑haul flights when availability and pricing are favourable

Other Transfer Bonuses We’ve Seen

RBC has also offered transfer bonuses to:

  • Cathay Pacific Asia Miles
  • American Airlines AAdvantage

Again, these are valuable because transfer bonuses are relatively rare in Canada. Having a bank program that occasionally boosts your airline conversions gives you leverage.

If you are patient and strategic, Avion points can stretch significantly further than their face value.

Is This Welcome Bonus Worth It?

If you:

  • Want flexible airline transfer options
  • Value the ability to move points strategically
  • Are comfortable waiting for transfer bonuses
  • Fly partners like British Airways, American Airlines, or WestJet

Then this elevated offer is strong.

At 55,000 points, you are getting close to the best publicly available Avion bonuses we typically see.

Final Thoughts

The RBC Avion Visa Infinite Card remains one of the most versatile travel credit cards available to Canadians. The increased welcome bonus simply makes the math more compelling.

As always, the real value comes from understanding how to maximize your points.
