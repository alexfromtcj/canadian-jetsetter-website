WestJet and Triangle Rewards Are Now Linked — Here's What It Means for You

If you fly WestJet and shop at Canadian Tire, SportChek, or Mark's, this one is worth paying attention to. WestJet Rewards and Triangle Rewards have officially linked up, and as of March 25, 2026, all it takes is connecting your two accounts to start double-earning. Here is a breakdown of what the partnership actually means and whether it is worth your time.

What Is the Partnership?

Two trusted Canadian loyalty programs — Triangle Rewards and WestJet Rewards — have teamed up to let members earn across both ecosystems simultaneously. Once your accounts are linked, you earn WestJet points when shopping at participating Triangle stores AND Canadian Tire Money when booking WestJet flights. Neither earn rate replaces the other — they stack on top.

How Earning Works at Triangle Stores

Linked WestJet Rewards members earn 1 WestJet point for every $2 spent at participating Triangle Rewards retailers including Canadian Tire, SportChek, Mark's, Party City, Pro Hockey Life and more. This is earned on top of your regular Canadian Tire Money — there is no reduction to your existing Triangle earning rate.

Points are awarded in $2 increments, so a $9 purchase earns 4 WestJet points.

How Earning Works on WestJet Bookings

Linked Triangle Rewards members earn 0.4% in Canadian Tire Money on eligible WestJet purchases including flights, vacation packages, baggage fees and seat selection. If you pay with a Triangle Mastercard, that rate increases to 1% in Canadian Tire Money on WestJet purchases.

Converting WestJet Points to Canadian Tire Money

Linked members can convert WestJet points into Canadian Tire Money at a rate of 120 WestJet points = $1 in Canadian Tire Money. A minimum of 2,400 WestJet points is required. Note that WestJet points are generally worth about 1 cent each toward travel, so converting to Canadian Tire Money at 120 points per dollar gives you slightly less value. Still, the flexibility is a nice option to have.
